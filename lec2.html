Lecture 2: Present Simple Tense

Formation

Positive Sentences

Subject + base form of verb (add -s/-es for he/she/it)

I work in a bank.
She works in a hospital.
They live in London.

Negative Sentences

Subject + do/does + not + base form of verb

I do not (don't) like coffee.
He does not (doesn't) play tennis.
We don't watch TV often.

Questions

Do/Does + subject + base form of verb

Do you speak English?
Does she work here?
Where do they live?

Uses

Habits and Repeated Actions

I usually get up at 7 o'clock.
She goes to the gym three times a week.
They always have breakfast together.
Note: Words that describe how often or when are often used (e.g. always, generally, normally, usually, often, sometimes, rarely, never, every day, every evening).

Permanent Situations

He works for a software company.
We live in Paris.

Accepted Truths and facts

The sun rises in the east.
Birds fly.
Two plus two equals four.

Note: The following words are often used: generally, mainly, normally, usually, traditionally.

Instructions and Directions

You go down to the traffic lights, then you turn left.
To start the programme, first you click on the icon on the desktop.

Stories And Talk About films, Books And Plays:

In the film, the tea lady falls in love with the Prime Minister.
Note: We also can use the past simple for stories but when we use the present simple it is like we are telling the story as it is occuring in front of us.
Note: For verbs ending in -ch, -sh, -ss, -x, or -o, add -es in the third person singular (he/she/it).

Common Time Expressions

  • always, usually, often
  • sometimes, occasionally
  • rarely, seldom
  • never
  • every day/week/month
  • on Mondays, at weekends
She always takes the bus to work.
We visit our grandparents every Sunday.
They don't often eat fast food.
