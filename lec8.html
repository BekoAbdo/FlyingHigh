Time Travel English Lesson

Mastering Future Tenses

If you had a Time Machine and you went to 1900, how could you prove that you are from the future?

Imagine you and your friends are planning a trip. Each of you knows a different part of the plan. Through your conversations, we can see how to use different future tenses in English.

Scene 1 ‚Äì The Travel Plan

Two of you know the full plan. One person knows nothing. One person joined the meeting but left before the final decision.

Stranger: "Where are you going?"

Stranger: "Where will you stay there?"

No knowledge: "We will stay in a hotel."

will (no arrangements yet)

Not sure, but intention exists: "We are going to stay in a hotel."

going to (intention, arrangements not complete)

Full plan confirmed: "We are staying in a hotel."

present continuous (arrangements done)

Full plan confirmed: "We will be staying in a hotel."

future continuous (arrangements done, action in progress)

Scene 2 ‚Äì The Weather

Two friends speak about the weather at their destination.

Opinion: "It will be extremely hot there."

will for personal opinion

Evidence: "It is going to be extremely hot there."

going to for future with evidence (weather forecast)

Scene 3 ‚Äì The Leader's Idea

Leader: "Why don't you write down these ideas?"

Friend: "Ok, I will start writing."

will (decision at the moment of speaking)

Scene 4 ‚Äì Time and Discipline

Strict friend: "The plane takes off at 7 a.m., don't be late."

present simple for official schedules

Scene 5 ‚Äì A Sudden Addition

Friend: "I want to visit the gaming tour. They will give the winner a prize."

will for facts in the future

Scene 6 ‚Äì The Disliked Son

Father: "Tomorrow I want you to visit your cousin."

Son: "I can't. Tomorrow I will be traveling to Paris."

future continuous (long action in the future)

Next morning...

Mother: "Where are you going dressed like that?"

Son: "I am about to visit Paris, Mom."

about to (something about to happen)

Mother: "Stay here!"

Son: "I am not about to go anywhere."

negative about to = no intention at all
