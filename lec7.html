Lecture 7: State Verbs

Definition

Verbs that itself are a general truth rather than something temporary. These verbs describe thoughts, feelings, senses, possession and description.
Since they are telling facts we use the present simple normally with them

I think we should tell her.

Examples

thoughts

agree, assume, believe, disagree, forget, hope, know, regret, remember, suppose, think, understand

Feelings

adore, despise, dislike, enjoy, feel, hate, like, love, mind, prefer, want.

Sense

feel, hear, see, smell, taste.

possession

have , own , belong

description

appear, contain, look, look like, mean, resemble, seem, smell, sound, taste, weigh
Note: Some state verbs can be used in the continuous form when the meaning is temporary. Compare:
What are you thinking about? (now)
I think you should tell her exactly what happened. (my opinion, so not temporary)
