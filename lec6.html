Lecture 6:Comparison and Superaltive

Comparison

To compare between two things.

Subject + is/am/are + adjective + er + than + Subject

Maraj is Taller than me.
He is Stronger than her.

Negative Sentences

Subject + is/am/are + not + adjective + er + than + Subject

Maraj is not Taller than me.
He is not Stronger than her.

Questions

am/is/are + Subject + adjective + er + than + subject

are you stronger than her?
Is maraj taller than me ?

Notes

Note: Adjecives that ends with (y) like: shy , we change the y to i then we add the er like: shy become shier.
Note: Adjecives that ends with (e) like: fine , we only add (r): fine become finer.
Note: Adjecives that have more than two syllables like (Dangerous - Important)we do not add er we just write the word (More) before it : Imoortant become more important.

Superaltive

To show somthing is the most or the best in a group

Subject + is/am/are + the + adjective + est

Maraj is the youngest.
He is the Strongest here.

Negative Sentences

Subject + is/am/are + not + the + adjective + est

Maraj is not the oldest.
He is not the biggest here.

Questions

am/is/are + Subject + the + adjective + est

are you the strongest?
Is Ahmed the tallest in the class ?

Notes

Note: Adjecives that ends with (y) like: shy , we change the y to i then we add the est like: shy become shiest.
Note: Adjecives that ends with (e) like: fine , we only add (st): fine become finest.
Note: Adjecives that have more than two syllables like (Dangerous - Important)we do not add est we just write the word (Most) before it : Imoortant become Most important.
Note: Adjecives that have more two syllables and ends with (er , y , ow , le , y) like (Clever - Friendly)we can add er and est when talking informally , or use more and most when we have to talk formally.
Note: Irragular Adjactives(Do not follow regular rules) :
good - better than - the best
bad - worse than- the worst
far - further - the farthest
littel - less than - the lowest
many/much - more than - the most
