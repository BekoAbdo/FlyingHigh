Lecture 5: Present Continuous Tense

Formation

Positive Sentences

Subject + is/am/are + verb + ing

I am watching the movie.
They are playing outside.

Negative Sentences

Subject + is/am/are + not + verb + ing

I am not talking to you.
He is not playing with us.

Questions

am/is/are + Subject + verb + ing

are you enjoying this?
am I teaching you?

Uses

Temporary Situations

I'm studying really hard for my exams.
My cousin is living in Thailand at the moment. (= he doesn't normally live there)
Note: I live in Rabak.
I am living in Rabak.
the first one means you live in Rabak Permanent. the secound means you are just living for a temporary reason.

Actions Happening At The Moment Of Speaking

I'm waiting for my friends.
I am writing.

Trends or Changing Situations

The Internet is making it easier for people to stay in touch with each other.
The price of petrol is rising dramatically.

Things That happen More Often Than Expected To show: Envy , Complaining , Criticise

My mum's always saying I don't help enough! (complaint).
He's always visiting exciting places! (envy)
Note: The present continuous is not normally used with state verbs because the meaning of the verb itself is a general truth rather than something temporary. These verbs describe thoughts, feelings, senses, possession and description.
